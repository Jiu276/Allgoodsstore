How to Choose the Right Running Shoes

Know Your Foot Type

  • Neutral: Normal arch, even wear pattern
  • Overpronation: Low arch, inner shoe wear
  • Supination: High arch, outer shoe wear

Visit a specialty running store for gait analysis.

Get the Right Fit

  • Shop in the afternoon when feet are swollen
  • Leave thumb's width of space in toe box
  • Ensure heel doesn't slip
  • Consider going up half a size

Match Shoe to Surface

  • Road: Smooth outsole, lighter weight
  • Trail: Aggressive tread, protection
  • Track: Minimal cushioning, lightweight
  • Treadmill: Less durable outsole acceptable

Replace Regularly

Track your mileage and replace shoes every:

  • 300-500 miles for regular trainers
  • 150-300 miles for racing shoes
  • 400-600 miles for trail shoes

Look for worn tread, compressed midsole, or uneven wear.
