Smart Watch Buying Guide

Compatibility

Ensure your smartwatch works with your phone. Apple Watches only work with iPhones, while Wear OS watches work best with Android but also support iOS with limitations.

Battery Life

Consider your charging habits. Some watches last 1-2 days with always-on displays, while others can go 2 weeks with basic features. Balance features with battery needs.

Health Features

Decide which health metrics matter most: heart rate, GPS, sleep tracking, SpO2, ECG, or stress monitoring. More sensors typically mean higher prices.

Display Technology

AMOLED offers vibrant colors and true blacks but uses more battery. LCD is more efficient but less vibrant. Consider always-on display needs.

Fitness Tracking

Athletes should prioritize GPS accuracy, multiple sport modes, and advanced metrics. Casual users can save money with basic step and calorie tracking.

Water Resistance

Look for at least 5ATM (50m) water resistance for swimming. IP68 protects against dust and splashes but isn't suitable for swimming.
